About Me

Education:
BSc Physics, New York University Abu Dhabi

Research Interests: Galaxy Formation and Evolution, Numerical Astrophysics, Dark Matter, Large Scale Structure

My research is centered on the formation and evolution of galaxies, with a particular focus on understanding the dynamics that govern small-scale systems. A significant portion of my work involves studying dwarf galaxy formation, where I explore the role of dark matter in shaping galaxy properties. I am interested in using numerical simulations, such as Zoom-in Gasoline SPH simulations, to model the impact of alternative dark matter models on small-scale structure formation. These simulations allow me to examine the interaction between dark matter and baryons, employing a hydrodynamic treatment to capture the complex processes that drive galaxy evolution in beyond-CDM scenarios.

Research

Fuzzy Gasoline: We developed a suite of cosmological hydrodynamical simulations of dwarf galaxies using the Fuzzy-Gasoline code, adapted from Gasoline2 and AX-Gadget. This code enables the modeling of complex baryonic processes, such as star formation, gas cooling, and black hole dynamics, in addition to the Schrödinger-Poisson wave dynamics characteristic of fuzzy dark matter (FDM). Our simulations investigated the impact of FDM dynamics on galaxy formation, adhering to mass constraints imposed by cosmic microwave background (CMB) and Lyman-alpha forest measurements. We focused on the lower end of these constraints to enhance the observable effects of FDM. Notably, we observed a delay in star formation of approximately 1 Gyr in the low-mass limit of the FDM mass. However, other stellar, gas, and kinematic properties, as well as scaling relationships, exhibited no significant deviation from those predicted by cold dark matter (CDM) models.

We have submitted a paper on Fuzzy-Gasoline to Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society (MNRAS), and it is currently under review. The preprint version is available here: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.09733.

Fuzzy-HELLO: We are developing high-redshift (z ~ 3) Fuzzy-Gasoline simulations, modeled after the HELLO suite, itself derived from the NIHAO suite of simulations. The goal of this project is to explore Fuzzy-Gasoline systems at higher redshifts, incorporating additional physics, such as local photoionization feedback, black hole feedback, and updated modules for chemical enrichment, while achieving higher resolutions. This approach allows us to leverage the weaker baryonic feedback and the stronger FDM Quantum Potential present at these early times. As this situation would amplify the dark matter core-cusp problem evident in low-to-mid-mass, dark matter-dominated Fuzzy-Gasoline dwarf galaxy systems, we expect to observe remnants of this phenomenon in their observable properties, particularly in their star formation histories.

Summary

These movies highlight three distinctive characteristics of FDM galaxies:

  • Filamentary halo structures: The halos are dominated by filamentary structures, which likely act as key pathways regulating the inflow and outflow of gas and dark matter in the primary halo.
  • Delayed star formation: Both M_2 and L_2 galaxies begin forming stars roughly 1 Gyr later than their CDM counterparts.
  • Reduced small-scale structure: FDM galaxies exhibit significantly fewer sub-halos—small, gravitationally bound clumps of dark matter and gas—compared to their CDM counterparts.
